Background

ReHome is a software system designed as an online marketplace for buying and selling used furniture, providing a specialized platform to connect local buyers and sellers. The system allows sellers to create detailed listings, including photos, descriptions, and pricing, making it easy to showcase furniture items. Buyers can browse these listings with advanced filtering options, such as location, price range, condition, and furniture type, to find exactly what they need. A key feature of ReHome is its emphasis on security and trust, offering integrated payment protection and potentially escrow services to ensure safe and reliable transactions, reducing the risk of fraud. In addition to the buying and selling process, ReHome introduces unique features to improve the user experience, such as a virtual room placement tool that allows buyers to visualize how the furniture would look in their home. This system addresses the shortcomings of general-purpose platforms by focusing on the specific needs of the used furniture market, making it easier, safer, and more efficient for users to buy and sell items.

Use Cases

There are 2 main types of intended users for the ReHome platform. One type of user would be the sellers who are interested in selling their furniture to others. The second are users who are interested in buying used furniture from safe sellers in their area. Below we examine the most common usage scenario for each target user.

Joey's Fresh Start

Joey is a senior in his last semester of college. He has been living alone in his apartment close to his university for the past 3 years. Joey is planning on moving to a different state after graduation for his new job. Since he plans to move so far away from his current city, Joey wants to start selling some of the furniture in his apartment before he moves out. He needs to be able to do the following using ReHome:

1 - List his furniture for sale on the platform

2 - Add specific details about his sale to the listing, such as price, furniture measurements, photos, and more

3 - Message potential buyers who are interested in any of his furniture pieces

4- Be able to vet/get assurance that the buyer he chooses is a real person and not a scammer

5- Safely complete the sale and have the money transferred over to him securely via the platform

Joey’s ultimate goal is to be able to sell his furniture to real people in his areas without fears that potential buyers are scammers who are trying to waste his time.

Rachel on a Budget

Rachel is finishing up her first year at university. She has secured an apartment for the upcoming school year and is planning to move in the week after the semester has ended. Rachel is a full time student and does not have the money to buy expensive, new furniture. She also does not have a car, so she wants to be able to find furniture listings close to her new apartment so she can have a friend help carry it over. As a result, she would like to use ReHome to find affordable used furniture for her new apartment. Rachel wants a platform that allows her to do the following:

1 - Search for furniture for sale on the platform

2 - Filter for specific details such as the type of furniture, the colors, the price range, and more

3 - Message potential sellers to ask about the price, when the item will be available for pickup, and other purchase details

4- Be able to vet/get assurance that the seller she chooses is a real person and not a scammer

5- Safely transfer the money with a cash back guarantee that she can get her money back in the case of a scam

In the end, Rachel hopes to find some good quality furniture pieces from other locals without a fear of being scammed by people who claim to have furniture for sale but just end up taking her money.

Requirements

Customer: Individuals or small businesses interested in buying and selling used furniture in a secure, specialized online marketplace. This includes users seeking convenience and trustworthiness in transactions, as well as a straightforward platform focused specifically on furniture.

Intended User Audience: Local buyers looking for specific furniture items and sellers who want an easy-to-use platform to list and sell their used furniture securely. Both buyers and sellers benefit from features like advanced filtering and secure transactions tailored to the furniture market.

Functional Requirements

FR-1: User Registration and Authentication

FR1.1: The system shall allow users to register an account by providing basic information (e.g., name, email, password).

FR1.2: The system shall require registered users to authenticate via login credentials (username and password) to access buying and selling features.

FR1.3: The system shall offer account recovery options (e.g., password reset via email) for users who forget their login credentials.

FR1.4: The system shall provide an option for users to authenticate their identity through a third-party service during registration or login.

FR-2: Listing Management for Sellers

FR2.1: The system shall allow sellers to create furniture listings by providing essential information, including photos, descriptions, and pricing.

FR2.2: The system shall support editing and updating listings, enabling sellers to modify item details as necessary.

FR2.3: The system shall allow sellers to delete listings from the marketplace when items are sold or no longer available.

FR-3: Search and Filtering for Buyers

FR3.1: The system shall allow buyers to search for furniture listings using keywords (e.g., “couch,” “dining table”).

FR3.2: The system shall offer advanced filtering options, including location, price range, item condition, and furniture type.

FR3.3: The system shall display search results that match the buyer's criteria, organized by relevance or proximity.

FR-4: Secure Transaction and Payment Processing

FR4.1: The system shall integrate secure payment options, like embedded payments within the platform, to facilitate transactions between buyers and sellers.

FR4.2: The system shall provide payment protection features, such as escrow or similar services, to ensure transaction security and build trust between buyers and sellers.

FR4.3: The system shall notify both parties (buyer and seller) upon successful transaction completion.

FR-5: Virtual Room Placement Tool

FR5.1: The system shall provide a virtual room placement feature that allows buyers to upload room dimensions or use a real-time camera view to visualize how selected furniture items will fit within their space.

FR5.2: The system shall allow buyers to save and adjust room placements to explore different furniture arrangements.

FR-6: User Communication and Notifications

FR6.1: The system shall allow buyers and sellers to communicate through an in-platform messaging system to coordinate purchases and delivery details.

FR6.2: The system shall send notifications to users for important events, such as new messages, listing updates, or transaction completions.

FR-7: User Profile and History Management

FR7.1: The system shall allow users to view and edit their profile information, including personal details and preferences.

FR7.2: The system shall keep a history of each user’s transactions (purchases and sales) for reference and record-keeping.

FR-8: Feedback and Rating System

FR8.1: The system shall allow buyers and sellers to provide feedback on transactions to enhance transparency and trust within the marketplace.

FR8.2: The system shall display user ratings and reviews, helping other users assess the reliability of buyers and sellers.

Non-Functional Requirements

NFR-1: Performance: ReHome should efficiently handle high volumes of concurrent users browsing, listing, and completing transactions. Listings on ReHome must load within 2 seconds, and images should upload in under 5 seconds to ensure a smooth experience for both buyers and sellers.

NFR-2: Scalability: ReHome must be scalable to accommodate an increasing number of users, listings, and transactions. As ReHome grows, it should continue to function smoothly, even during spikes in traffic, such as special sales or promotions.

NFR-3: Security: ReHome must prioritize user data security by implementing secure authentication mechanisms, encrypting sensitive information such as payment details, and ensuring compliance with data protection laws like GDPR. ReHome must also include fraud prevention features and safeguards for safe transactions. ReHome also verifies the identities of every user to ensure they are real people and not impersonating anyone.

NFR-4: Availability: ReHome must maintain high availability, ensuring at least 99.9% uptime. In case of system failure, ReHome must have robust backup and disaster recovery processes in place to quickly restore services without major disruption.

NFR-5: Usability: ReHome’s user interface should be designed for simplicity and ease of use, making it intuitive for both buyers and sellers. The navigation, filtering options, and listing creation tools on ReHome should be clear and easy to interact with, providing a pleasant user experience for all demographics.

NFR-6: Maintainability: ReHome should adopt a modular architecture to facilitate easy maintenance, including updates, bug fixes, and the addition of new features. Developers should be able to modify and expand ReHome’s system without causing significant downtime or breaking existing functionality.

NFR-7: Compatibility: ReHome must be compatible with a variety of platforms, including different web browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari) and mobile devices (iOS, Android). Users should have a consistent experience accessing ReHome across all devices.

NFR-8: Reliability: ReHome must be reliable in processing transactions without errors. Payment processing and notifications (such as order confirmations) on ReHome should be dependable to ensure trust and minimize potential disputes between buyers and sellers.

NFR-9: Responsiveness: ReHome must be responsive to user inputs, including searches, filtering options, and interactive tools like the virtual room placement feature. The system should minimize latency to provide users with real-time feedback as they browse or interact with listings.

NFR-10: Legal Compliance: ReHome must comply with all relevant e-commerce, consumer protection, and tax laws. It should ensure that all transactions on ReHome follow applicable regulations to protect both the business and its users from legal risks.

NFR-11: Fault Tolerance: ReHome should be able to quickly recover if a specific component in the system fails. As it has a modular architecture, if one fails it should not affect the others, and ReHome should also remain operational.

High Level Design

Our team chose to use a microservice architecture to develop our application. In this design, the business logic is separated into microservices that handle different functions within the app. This overall design can be separated into tiers.

The first tier is the client tier. The client is the frontend layer and will contain the mobile application and its user interface. The client will send users requests to the server in the form of API calls and display the responses from the server.

The second tier is the API. The API will delegate the requests received from the client to the appropriate microservice. It will then pass the results of these tasks back to the client.

The third and final tier is the data tier. This backend layer will contain the database servers that store and manage data. Each microservice will have its own private database. The application server will use a relational database, like MySQL or PostgreSQL.

Conclusion

ReHome is a useful system for those who want a centralized platform to navigate through the process of buying and selling used furniture. Its unique features like a virtual room placement tool and integrated payment system make it stand out from other used furniture marketplaces available online. The system takes advantage of microservices architecture to split each important function into its own component. This allows ReHome to maintain availability during high loads of usage, and ensures it can be scalable over time as the platform grows. In the case of one module failing, fault tolerance can ensure the system will remain operational.

In order to support its unique features, ReHome employs the technique of hand-held augmented reality and embedded payment systems. Having hand-held augmented reality allows users to visualize the furniture in their room for the Virtual Room Placement Tool. This feature is a game changer for users who are interested in buying a product but want to ensure it will be a perfect fit in their room. Unlike many other used furniture platforms, ReHome will take advantage of embedded payment systems to allow users the option to pay for their purchase within the app itself. This helps simplify the process of purchasing furniture and provides an online receipt confirming that the money was safely transferred. Embedded payment systems provide encryption to handle sensitive financial data, and they also use API calls to verify valid payment methods, making it a safe choice for secure payment transactions.

By combining these various design decisions, ReHome is able to fulfill its requirements and provide a beneficial platform for those who are interested in either buying or selling used furniture to locals in their area.
